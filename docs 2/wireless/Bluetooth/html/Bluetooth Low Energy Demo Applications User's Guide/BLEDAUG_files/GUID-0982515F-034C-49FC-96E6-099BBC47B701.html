QN9090 and K32W061 end of OTA image transfer

For OTA upgrade in internal flash (default behavior of the OTAP client demo application), the flash remapping mechanism is used. At the end of an image transfer the OTA Support will indicate to the ROM code to select the second partition. At the next reboot, the new image will be chosen. In order to support the OTA in internal fiash, the two regions containing the application and the update section must have a granularity of 8 KB.

On QN9090 and K32W061, it is also possible to support storage of the updated image in the external flash. To support OTA on external flash a Secondary Stage Bootloader (SSBL) is required. When OTA in external flash is used, at the end of an image transfer, the OTA Support will store information in the PSECT to indicate to the SSBL that a new image is available in the external flash. At the next reboot, the SSBL will overse some checks of the image stored on the external flash before copying the image to its targeted location.
